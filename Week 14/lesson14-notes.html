Creating an attractive and effective website doesn't require building a complex hierarchy of webpages, and, depending on the content, could be accomplished with a single webpage. Having a single page for your website has the advantage of fast loading times, fast navigation, the ability to save the whole page to a user's local machine, and simple printing jobs, since all the content is on one page. However, in most cases, companies and readers organize their website into short, quickly-read pages that don't require scrolling and has a navigation bar to connect the user to any other webpage on the site. The fundamental goal of a website is to provide visibility to an individual or organization and to act as a portal to the website's content. In order to make your website memoriable, it should have a visual theme that is distinct from other websites, and that theme should be consistent across the webpages of the site.

Organizing your website, including your navigation bar, is especially important for the largest websites on the internet, such as amazon.com. While there many ways to organize your website, there are several guidelines to follow when doing so: deciding where the important content areas are and how to organize them, using visual cues and changes to help the user orient themselves on the current webpage, and to try to limits your navigation links to seven items or less, or otherwise split them into subcategories of seven items or less each. Besides organizing the website itself, another aspect of managing a website is SEO, or search engine optimization. Some important factors that improves your ranking in search engines are the following: create interesting content on the site in a variety of media, make navigation easy, and optimize your site for mobile devices.

When it comes to creating interesting content, it's important that it is full of keywords, is concise and comprehensive, is well structured in HTML, has good navigation to other pages, and has other interesting media, like tables, figures and graphs. If you're really committed to SEO, you should also focus on putting LSI, or latent semantic indexing, keywords in your website. While search engines can navigate your website faster than a human, you should still make your navigation easy and intuitive, while avoiding Javascript for navigation if possible. What's also recommended is that you not only have links to other webpages on your site, but also links to other parts of any given webpage for easy navigation to desired content. Since search engines are prioritizing mobile devices for their web results, it is important to make sure your webpages are optimized for mobile devices. Guidelines to follow to create mobile-friendly webpages is to make them responsive, keep webpages as small as possible in terms of file size, and to retain content regardless of device, especially mobile devices.

Another important factor to managing a website is to write maintainable code. A major part of adhering to maintenance is documenting it with comments and indenting code to easily see its hierarchy. Version control is also a consideration for maintaining code for your website because it allows you to revert to a previous version of your webpage code. Other benefits of version control include commiting only certain files of your code, branching your code to have more than one version to experiment with, a detailed log of changes from version to version, and both contributing to an open source repository or having user contribute code to your repository, if it's open source. There are several version control sites out there, with the most popular being Github. An option for customizing your website to your needs is using HTML and CSS frameworks like Bootstrap so you can cut down on development time and develop more sophisticated website with simpler code.
